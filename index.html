We're more than just a school robotics club, we're are a comunity of like-minded individuals who are passionate about robotics and technology. We are a team of students from The Gryphon School, Sherborne. We are a part of the FIRST Tech Challenge UK (FTC), in which we participate in the South-West Regional Qualifiers which take place at Bournemouth University.

Our team of hardworking and dedicated students and mentors are what drive the team forward to compete in the FIRST Tech Challenge. We take great pride in our hard work and dedication to help us achieve our goals.

Great Success At The UK National Finals!
As it is the team's rookie year we did not expect to qualify for the UK National Finals, but we did! And you sure bet we made the most of it! Although we did not come home with a trophy or award we did come home with a 3D Printer due to completing a competion. To add to this success the team qualified 4th out of 32 teams within our division (Div 2 - Pacific) which meant we got to choose our alliance partners for the knockout rounds. We can confidently say we had a great alliance partner with a great team chemistry as we allowed them to focus on specimines whilst we focused on scoring samples in the buckets. This allowed us to get all the way to the quarter-finals of the whole competition (Semis of Division 2) where the team agonisingly lost 124-125. However, we are all very proud of ourselves as a team, due to it being our rookie year in FTC we managed to get so far within the competition and redifine what being a team in FTC means!

OUR MISSION
THE MISSION

As a team, we recognize the importance of having a clear and purposeful mission to guide our outreach efforts within the local community. A defined mission not only provides direction but also amplifies our ability to share our identity and goals with those around us. Prior to our formation, STEM-related opportunities were virtually non-existent in our area. Through our participation in the FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC), we’ve discovered a deep passion for STEM, and we are committed to sharing that enthusiasm with others.

Community outreach has become a cornerstone of our FTC journey. By engaging with our school and community events, we aim to raise awareness about STEM and inspire the next generation of innovators. These outreach efforts allow us to highlight the possibilities within science, technology, engineering, and mathematics - fields that are increasingly vital in today’s technology-driven world.

THE ALGORYPHON IDE PROGRAMME
The Algoryphon IDE Programme is our dedicated initiative to inspire and educate young minds in technology-focused subjects. In a world where nearly every industry relies on technological proficiency, we believe it is essential to equip the next generation with the skills and confidence to thrive.

Our programme is built on three core pillars:
INSPIRE
We aim to ignite curiosity and passion for STEM by sharing our own journey and enthusiasm. Through interactive demonstrations and storytelling, we hope to show young people that anyone can succeed in STEM with the right mindset and support.
DESIGN
We encourage creativity and innovation by helping students explore the design process. Whether it’s building simple robots, brainstorming solutions to real-world problems, or participating in hands-on workshops, we want to empower young minds to think boldly and take risks in their ideas.

FROM FIRST

Every artifact we uncover holds a story. Each tool, each innovation, each work of art connects us to the people and ideas that came before us. Using STEM skills and teamwork, today we can dig deeper into discoveries than ever before.

Welcome to FIRST® AGE™ presented by Qualcomm, our 2025-2026 robotics season inspired by archaeology. What will you uncover? Join us for an experience for the ages.


Learn more about the theme here (FIRST Inspires Website)

The range of opportunities that an education in STEM (science, technology, engineering, and math) offers young people is wider than ever. FIRST® helps kids uncover their futures using a winning formula of inspiration, hands-on learning, team-based problem solving, mentorship, and celebration.

More Than Robots®, we are a life-changing global community of mentors, educators, volunteers, sponsors, parents, and alumni who are dedicated to helping all young people develop the skills, confidence, and resilience to succeed.

Exciting, hands-on FIRST programs have a proven and lasting impact on STEM learning and interest in students. FIRST participants work as teams in creative learning environments to solve an annual, themed robotics challenge.
FIRST® Tech Challenge (ages 12-18 / grades 7-12) students work together with their mentors to design and build robots to compete in a dynamic and exciting challenge released every September.

Teams program classroom-scale robots to follow autonomous commands before student drivers take control in two-on-two matches. On and off the field, students develop STEM skills, engage in community outreach, practice engineering innovation, and build confidence to help them succeed.

Starting September 6, 2025, FIRST Tech Challenge teams will investigate the power of the world’s artifacts and unlock mysteries in a new challenge, DECODE™ presented by RTX.
